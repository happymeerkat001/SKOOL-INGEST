# Facebook-Centric Inbound Lead Qualification Pipeline

## 1. Intake Layer — how inbound data is ingested

**Channels**
- Primary: Facebook Marketplace room listings. Secondary: FB groups, Roomies, SpareRoom. Operator states total dependence: "If I lose my account, my business goes down. I rely on Facebook only." [Systems to Operate like a Coliving Pro 0513, ~2181s]
- Conversion baseline: ~100 leads → ~2 placements.

**Listing strategy**
- Rooms sometimes posted in furniture category ("bed") instead of rentals — looser description rules vs. FB's fair-housing AI scanning rental listings. [FB Marketing everything, ~331-356s]
- Posting windows: peak engagement 5:30-7:30 pm; advertise only ~20th-30th of month to dodge competition crunch. [FB Marketing everything, ~431s, ~4756s]

**Speed-to-lead (the core intake SLA)**
- Reply within 15-20 seconds; response speed is "the number one metric Facebook uses to push you to the top of the page," especially for new accounts. [FB Marketing everything, ~1443-1480s; AI-Native Marketing 0429, ~1845s]
- Volume: ~150 inbound messages/day at full operation. [FB Marketplace Live Audit 0304, ~2422s]
- Tooling: preloaded-message keyboard app ("World War" [sic — likely a text-expander keyboard]) for instant canned replies. [FB Marketing everything, ~198-209s, ~1544s]
- Mature state: AI handles first contact entirely — "All the messages you're seeing on Facebook Marketplace, that's my AI talking." [FB Marketplace-AI SOPs 0401, ~4479s] Lead Acquisition Agent "engages prospects 24/7... pre-qualifies the leads and schedules the showings, and then it shows on my calendar." [AI Automations Intro 0225, ~1491-1510s]

## 2. Analysis Layer — how leads are processed/qualified

**Three-message qualification funnel** (fixed script, no improvisation):
1. **Msg 1** — availability + hard constraints: single occupancy (one adult), no pets. "Most people don't make it after message number one." [FB Marketing everything, ~1562-1584s]
2. **Msg 2** — location/commute fit + move-in date: "This house is located one or two minutes from this address. Is that close to your work? When do you want to come see it?" [~2019s]
3. **Msg 3** — showing booked. "On the first three messages they are going to tell me everything I need to know." [My SOPs tenant screening 1015, ~2975s]

**Qualification logic**
- Binary disqualifiers, no scoring: kids/pregnancy, pets, felonies, income < 2.5x rent. [FB Marketing everything, ~3903s, ~6274s; Tenant Acquisition 0111, ~1967s, ~3436s]
- Self-disqualification by design — never say "no" directly; constraints in the script make unfit leads drop out themselves (also reduces report/review risk on the FB account). [FB Marketing everything, ~3089s, ~3450s]
- Funnel diagnostics: clicks-without-messages vs. messages-without-showings used to localize whether listing copy or messaging is broken. [~2767s]

**AI prompt/template system**
- 333 standardized response templates ("SLPs") generated by feeding ~9,000 historical tenant messages (3 yrs, Google Voice export consolidated to HTML via Python) into Gemini — 36-hour analysis run. [AI Automations Intro 0225, ~1937-1972s]
- LLM choice: Gemini preferred over ChatGPT for instruction-following ("ChatGPT wasn't getting the results I wanted" — added unsolicited suggestions). Two agents: tenant-facing concierge + lead-gen. [AI Automations Intro 0225, ~1366s, ~4137s]
- Agent router coordinating 15-16 single-mission agents. [~1550s]

**Handoff**
- Qualified → showing (fixed windows, e.g. 5-7 pm daily) → on-site application ("have a seat in the living room, I just sent the application to your phone") → background check (SmartMove + second FCRA-approved provider via Baselane) → lease auto-trigger + onboarding. [FB Marketing everything, ~2111-2536s; Systems 0513, ~1678s]

## 4. Compliance & Reliability practices

**Platform ToS / fair housing**
- FB AI continuously scans listing descriptions for discrimination-act violations; operator rewrites copy to comply (and category-shifts to furniture where rules are looser — note: this is a ToS gray area, not a best practice). [FB Marketing everything, ~331s]
- Tenant denial only on FCRA-approved data sources, never on ad-hoc online findings. [Systems 0513, ~1672-1705s]
- Compliance research habit: cross-check answers across ChatGPT + Gemini + Claude because "every LLM gives a different answer." [Systems 0513, ~1956s]

**Session/account reliability**
- FB account periodically disabled/shadow-limited (e.g. zero messages for 2 days); only known recovery: share listing + collect positive reviews. No real prevention mechanism — single-platform dependence is the acknowledged top business risk. [Systems 0513, ~2153-2204s]
- Non-response to inbound messages ~3x triggers account review — another reason for the speed-to-lead SLA. [FB Marketing everything, ~3543s]

**Data integrity / failure handling**
- AI timing unreliable for scheduled sends (6 pm brief arrives 5:52-5:56); time-critical steps (onboarding delivery) have failed and required manual apology/refund fallback. Human stays in the loop for irreversible steps. [AI Automations Intro 0225, ~55s; FB Marketplace-AI SOPs, ~517-543s]
- No formal backup procedure discussed; Google Drive is the de-facto system of record. Spreadsheet hygiene flagged as a known gap when absorbing new properties. [Systems 0513, ~2270s]
